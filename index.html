Guide
How to log clean data and read Training Load
Input

Logging workouts

Use one row per exercise. For strength work, enter weight in kg and reps for each set. When you add another set, the app repeats the last weight and reps so you can adjust only what changed.

For bodyweight movements like pull-ups, dips, push-ups, leg raises, planks, and sit-ups, leave weight empty unless extra load was added.

Metric

Training Load

Training Load estimates total strength demand across weighted and bodyweight exercises. It is used for mixed charts, weekly insight, and body-part distribution.

Weighted: weight × reps × sets
Bodyweight: (65 kg estimate × exercise factor + added weight) × reps × sets

Traditional Volume remains useful for weighted exercise details where kg × reps is the clearest view.

Method

Bodyweight estimates

The current bodyweight estimate is 65 kg. This is a fixed app default for now, not your saved personal bodyweight.

Pull-up example: 65 × 0.95 × 5 reps = 309 kg Training Load
Push-up example: 65 × 0.65 × 10 reps = 423 kg Training Load

These factors are directional. They help compare training trends, not measure exact force.

Method

Muscle maps

Each mapped exercise has primary muscles, secondary muscles, and a load split across involved muscle groups.

For unmapped exercises, the app infers a safe type and muscle split where possible, then logs an internal console warning for review.

Caveat

Unilateral work

Single-side movements such as split squats, lunges, cable kickbacks, and single-leg RDLs are flagged as unilateral.

The app preserves your current logging behavior and does not double-count unless future data clearly records both sides.

Cardio

Separate from strength

Cardio is tracked separately as cardio effort using duration and intensity inputs such as speed, level, or elevation.

Cardio does not contribute to strength muscle-load charts unless it is explicitly marked as conditioning in the data.
